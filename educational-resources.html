Stock Market Basics

What is the Stock Market?

The stock market is a place where shares of publicly traded companies are bought and sold. It's a crucial part of the financial system that enables companies to raise capital and investors to participate in the financial success of these companies.

  • Primary Market: Where new stocks are first issued
  • Secondary Market: Where existing stocks are traded
  • Market Participants: Investors, traders, brokers

Investment Basics

Before investing in stocks, understand these fundamental concepts:

  • Demat Account: Required for holding shares electronically
  • Trading Account: Needed for buying and selling shares
  • KYC Requirements: Identity and address proof

Risk Management

Essential risk management principles for new investors:

  • Never invest more than you can afford to lose
  • Diversify your portfolio across sectors
  • Start with blue-chip stocks for stability
  • Use stop-loss orders to limit potential losses

Basic Chart Reading

Understanding basic chart elements:

  • Candlestick Charts: Show price movement patterns
  • Support Levels: Price levels where buying pressure exceeds selling
  • Resistance Levels: Price levels where selling pressure exceeds buying
  • Volume: Indicates trading activity and trend strength
